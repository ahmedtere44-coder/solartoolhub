How to size a solar system: the complete guide

Sizing a solar system is really six connected decisions — array, battery, inverter, charge controller, strings, and cables. This guide explains how each one is calculated and links you straight to the tool for it.

"What size solar system do I need?" is the question behind almost every solar project — and the honest answer is that it's not one number, it's a chain of connected calculations. Each component depends on the ones before it, so getting the order right is what separates a balanced system from an expensive mismatch. This guide walks through every sizing decision and points you to the exact calculator for each.

Start with your energy needs

Before sizing any hardware, you need one number: how much energy you use, in kilowatt-hours. Everything downstream scales from it. Guess this and every other figure inherits the error, which is why the very first step is an honest tally of your consumption. From there, the sizing chain flows naturally through each component below.

Why the order matters

Each step feeds the next. Your energy usage sizes the solar array; the array and your overnight needs size the battery; your peak load sizes the inverter; the array's current sizes the charge controller; and the whole layout determines your string configuration and cable sizes. Jump around and the numbers won't line up. Follow the chain and every component is matched to the rest.

Frequently asked questions

How do I size a solar power system?

Work out your energy needs, then size the array to meet them, the battery to store overnight energy, the inverter for your peak load, the charge controller to match the array, and the cables to carry current safely. Each has a dedicated calculator above; do them in order.

What size solar system do I need?

It depends on your electricity usage and local sun hours. Instead of guessing a round number, start from your kilowatt-hours and let the panel calculator turn that plus your location into an exact array size.

Should I size the panels or the battery first?

Size your energy needs first, then the battery, then the panels. They depend on each other, so always start from a solid daily-consumption figure.
