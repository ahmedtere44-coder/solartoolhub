Solar power's biggest appeal is often the money it saves — but the environmental payoff is just as real, and it's easy to quantify. Every unit of electricity your panels produce is a unit that didn't have to come from burning coal or gas, so it directly avoids the carbon dioxide that would have been released. This calculator turns your system's output into the CO₂ it keeps out of the atmosphere, plus relatable equivalents.

How the calculation works

The core formula is simple:

CO₂ saved = yearly production (kWh) × grid emission factor (kg/kWh)

If you don't know your yearly production, the calculator estimates it from your system size and local peak sun hours (system kW × sun hours × 365, with a small real-world performance allowance). Then it multiplies by your grid's emission factor to get the CO₂ avoided.

What's a grid emission factor?

This is the average CO₂ released per kilowatt-hour on your electricity grid, and it varies a lot by country. Coal-heavy grids sit around 0.7–0.9 kg per kWh, mixed grids around 0.4–0.5, and grids rich in hydro or nuclear can be under 0.1. The default here is 0.45, close to the world average — but if you know your country's figure, enter it for a more accurate result. The dirtier your grid, the more CO₂ your solar actually offsets.

The equivalents explained

Tonnes of CO₂ can feel abstract, so the calculator also shows two everyday comparisons. Tree-years uses the rough figure that a mature tree absorbs about 21 kg of CO₂ per year — so the number tells you how many trees working for a year it would take to match your savings. Car kilometres uses about 0.12 kg of CO₂ per km for an average petrol car, showing how much driving your savings offset. Both are estimates meant to make the number tangible, not exact accounting.

Why it adds up over time

A single year's saving is meaningful, but the real impact is cumulative. Because panels last decades, the lifetime figure over 25 years is where solar's environmental value really shows — often tens of tonnes of CO₂ avoided from one rooftop system. That's the number worth sharing.

Frequently asked questions

How much CO₂ does a solar system save?

It depends on production and how dirty the grid is. Each kWh of solar avoids roughly 0.4–0.7 kg of CO₂. A 5 kW system making ~7,500 kWh/year avoids roughly 3–5 tonnes a year, or 75–130 tonnes over 25 years.

How is solar CO₂ saving calculated?

Multiply your production in kWh by your grid's emission factor (kg CO₂ per kWh). That solar energy replaces fossil-fuel electricity, so the result is the CO₂ avoided.

What is a grid emission factor?

The average CO₂ per kWh on your grid. Coal-heavy ~0.7–0.9, mixed ~0.4–0.5, clean (hydro/nuclear) under 0.1. World average ≈ 0.45 kg per kWh.
